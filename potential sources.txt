Okay, here is a list of potential sources organized by the key subjects in your project outline. This list exceeds 50 sources and provides a mix of seminal papers, textbooks, survey articles, standards documents, and reputable online resources. This should serve as an excellent starting point for your research.

Note: Access to some journal articles or conference proceedings might require institutional subscriptions or purchase. Many university resources and ArXiv papers are freely available.

Organized List of Potential Sources (Starting Point)

I. Quantum Mechanics & Quantum Computing Fundamentals

Textbooks (Comprehensive QC):

Nielsen, M. A., & Chuang, I. L. (2010). Quantum Computation and Quantum Information: 10th Anniversary Edition. Cambridge University Press. (The standard reference textbook).

Mermin, N. D. (2007). Quantum Computer Science: An Introduction. Cambridge University Press. (More concise, focused on computation).

Kaye, P., Laflamme, R., & Mosca, M. (2007). An Introduction to Quantum Computing. Oxford University Press.

Yanofsky, N. S., & Mannucci, M. A. (2008). Quantum Computing for Computer Scientists. Cambridge University Press.

Rieffel, E. G., & Polak, W. H. (2011). Quantum Computing: A Gentle Introduction. MIT Press.

Quantum Mechanics Principles:
6. Feynman, R. P., Leighton, R. B., & Sands, M. (1965). The Feynman Lectures on Physics, Vol. III: Quantum Mechanics. Addison-Wesley. (Classic physics text).
7. Griffiths, D. J., & Schroeter, D. F. (2018). Introduction to Quantum Mechanics. Cambridge University Press. (Standard undergraduate QM textbook).
8. Arndt, M., Nairz, O., Vos-Andreae, J., Keller, C., Van Der Zouw, G., & Zeilinger, A. (1999). Wave–particle duality of C60 molecules. Nature, 401(6754), 680-682. (Experimental demonstration of duality for large molecules).
9. Einstein, A., Podolsky, B., & Rosen, N. (1935). Can Quantum-Mechanical Description of Physical Reality Be Considered Complete?. Physical Review, 47(10), 777. (The famous EPR paper questioning completeness, related to entanglement).
10. Bell, J. S. (1964). On the Einstein Podolsky Rosen paradox. Physics Physique Fizika, 1(3), 195. (Bell's theorem, foundational for understanding entanglement tests).
11. Heisenberg, W. (1927). Über den anschaulichen Inhalt der quantentheoretischen Kinematik und Mechanik. Zeitschrift für Physik, 43(3-4), 172-198. (Original paper on the Uncertainty Principle - German, English translations available).

Online Resources / Tutorials:
12. IBM Quantum Experience Documentation: https://quantum-computing.ibm.com/docs/ (Excellent practical guides, tutorials on Qiskit, quantum concepts).
13. Microsoft Quantum Documentation: https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/quantum/ (Resources for Q#, quantum concepts, algorithms).
14. Caltech Institute for Quantum Information and Matter (IQIM) Outreach: https://iqim.caltech.edu/outreach (Explanations of quantum concepts).
15. Quanta Magazine - Quantum Physics Section: https://www.quantamagazine.org/tag/quantum-physics/ (High-quality articles on recent developments).
16. Physics Stack Exchange: https://physics.stackexchange.com/ (Question/Answer forum for specific concepts).

II. Quantum Algorithms

Shor's Algorithm:
17. Shor, P. W. (1994). Algorithms for quantum computation: discrete logarithms and factoring. Proceedings 35th Annual Symposium on Foundations of Computer Science, 124-134. (The seminal paper).
18. Shor, P. W. (1997). Polynomial-Time Algorithms for Prime Factorization and Discrete Logarithms on a Quantum Computer. SIAM Journal on Computing, 26(5), 1484-1509. (Journal version).
19. Beckman, D., Chari, A. N., Devabhaktuni, S., & Preskill, J. (1996). Efficient networks for quantum factoring. Physical Review A, 54(2), 1034. (Implementation details/circuit design).

Grover's Algorithm:
20. Grover, L. K. (1996). A fast quantum mechanical algorithm for database search. Proceedings, 28th Annual ACM Symposium on the Theory of Computing, 212-219. (The seminal paper).
21. Grover, L. K. (1997). Quantum Mechanics Helps in Searching for a Needle in a Haystack. Physical Review Letters, 79(2), 325. (Journal version).
22. Boyer, M., Brassard, G., Høyer, P., & Tapp, A. (1998). Tight bounds on quantum searching. Fortschritte der Physik: Progress of Physics, 46(4‐5), 493-505. (Analysis of optimality).

III. Classical Cryptography

Textbooks:
23. Katz, J., & Lindell, Y. (2014). Introduction to Modern Cryptography. Chapman and Hall/CRC. (Widely used modern textbook).
24. Stallings, W. (2017). Cryptography and Network Security: Principles and Practice. Pearson. (Comprehensive, practical focus).
25. Menezes, A. J., Van Oorschot, P. C., & Vanstone, S. A. (1996). Handbook of Applied Cryptography. CRC Press. (The comprehensive reference, often called HAC).
26. Paar, C., & Pelzl, J. (2010). Understanding Cryptography: A Textbook for Students and Practitioners. Springer.

Seminal Papers / Standards:
27. Diffie, W., & Hellman, M. (1976). New directions in cryptography. IEEE Transactions on Information Theory, 22(6), 644-654. (Introduced public-key cryptography and key exchange).
28. Rivest, R. L., Shamir, A., & Adleman, L. (1978). A method for obtaining digital signatures and public-key cryptosystems. Communications of the ACM, 21(2), 120-126. (The RSA paper).
29. National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST). (2001). FIPS PUB 197: Advanced Encryption Standard (AES). https://csrc.nist.gov/publications/detail/fips/197/final
30. Koblitz, N. (1987). Elliptic curve cryptosystems. Mathematics of Computation, 48(177), 203-209. (Introduced ECC).
31. Miller, V. S. (1986). Use of elliptic curves in cryptography. Advances in Cryptology — CRYPTO ’85 Proceedings, 417-426. (Another foundational ECC paper).
32. National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST). FIPS PUB 180-4: Secure Hash Standard (SHS). https://csrc.nist.gov/publications/detail/fips/180-4/final (Defines SHA family).
33. National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST). FIPS PUB 202: SHA-3 Standard: Permutation-Based Hash and Extendable-Output Functions. https://csrc.nist.gov/publications/detail/fips/202/final
34. Kerckhoffs, A. (1883). La cryptographie militaire. Journal des sciences militaires, vol. IX, pp. 5–38, Jan. 1883, pp. 161–191, Feb. 1883. (Origin of Kerckhoffs's Principle - French).

IV. Impact of Quantum Computing on Cryptography

Surveys / Reports:
35. Mosca, M. (2018). Cybersecurity in an era with quantum computers: will we be ready?. EPJ Quantum Technology, 5(1), 1-15. (Often cited for timelines/risk). Updated versions/talks often available online.
36. Bernstein, D. J., & Lange, T. (2017). Post-quantum cryptography. Nature, 549(7671), 188-194. (Good overview of the threat and solutions).
37. European Union Agency for Cybersecurity (ENISA). Reports on Post-Quantum Cryptography. (Search ENISA website for latest reports, e.g., "Post-Quantum Cryptography: Current state and quantity recommendations"). https://www.enisa.europa.eu/
38. National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine. (2019). Quantum Computing: Progress and Prospects. National Academies Press. (Comprehensive report on the state of QC, including crypto impact). https://www.nap.edu/catalog/25196/quantum-computing-progress-and-prospects

Specific Impact Analysis:
39. Proos, J., & Zalka, C. (2003). Shor's discrete logarithm quantum algorithm for elliptic curves. Quantum Info. Comput., 3(4), 317-344. (Details on breaking ECC).
40. Grassl, M., Langenberg, B., Roetteler, M., & Steinwandt, R. (2016). Applying Grover's algorithm to AES: quantum resource estimates. Post-Quantum Cryptography, 29-43. Springer. (Analysis of resources needed for Grover attack on AES).

V. Quantum-Resistant Cryptography (PQC)

NIST PQC Standardization Process:
41. National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST). Post-Quantum Cryptography Project Website. https://csrc.nist.gov/projects/post-quantum-cryptography (Primary source for standards, reports, selected algorithms).
42. NIST Internal Report 8105: Report on Post-Quantum Cryptography (2016). https://csrc.nist.gov/publications/detail/nistir/8105/final (Initial report outlining the process).
43. NIST Status Reports on the PQC Standardization Process (Multiple Rounds). (Available on the NIST PQC website).
44. Alagic, G., et al. (2020). Status Report on the Second Round of the NIST Post-Quantum Cryptography Standardization Process. NISTIR 8309. https://csrc.nist.gov/publications/detail/nistir/8309/final (Example report - look for the latest).
45. The specific algorithm specifications selected by NIST (e.g., CRYSTALS-Kyber, CRYSTALS-Dilithium, Falcon, SPHINCS+). (Available on the NIST PQC website).

Surveys & Overviews of PQC Families:
46. Bernstein, D. J., Buchmann, J., & Dahmen, E. (Eds.). (2009). Post-Quantum Cryptography. Springer. (Early, but foundational book).
47. Alkim, E., Bindel, N., Buchmann, J., Dagdelen, Ö., & Pöppelmann, T. (2016). Post-quantum key exchange–a new hope. IACR Cryptology ePrint Archive, 2016/659. (Focuses on NewHope lattice scheme).
48. Peikert, C. (2016). A decade of lattice cryptography. Foundations and Trends® in Theoretical Computer Science, 10(4), 283-424. (Excellent survey of lattice-based crypto).
49. Hülsing, A. (2013). W-OTS+ – Shorter Signatures for Hash-Based Signature Schemes. Progress in Cryptology–AFRICACRYPT 2013, 173-190. (Example of hash-based signature research).
50. Moody, D., Alagic, G., Apon, D. C., Cooper, D. A., Dang, Q. H., Kelsey, J. M., ... & Peralta, R. C. (2023). Getting ready for post-quantum cryptography: exploring challenges in the migration to PQC. ACM Computing Surveys. (Recent survey on migration challenges).

Specific PQC Areas:
51. Micciancio, D., & Regev, O. (2009). Lattice-based cryptography. Post-quantum cryptography, 147-191. Springer.
52. Sendrier, N. (2017). Code-Based Cryptography: A Concise Overview. Journal of Mathematical Cryptology, 11(1), 3-11.
53. Ding, J., Gower, J. E., & Schmidt, D. S. (2006). Multivariate public key cryptosystems. Advances in Information Security, Vol 25. Springer.

VI. Quantum Key Distribution (QKD)

Seminal Papers / Protocols:
54. Bennett, C. H., & Brassard, G. (1984). Quantum cryptography: Public key distribution and coin tossing. Proceedings of IEEE International Conference on Computers, Systems and Signal Processing, 175-179. (The original BB84 protocol).
55. Ekert, A. K. (1991). Quantum cryptography based on Bell’s theorem. Physical Review Letters, 67(6), 661. (Entanglement-based QKD - E91 protocol).

Reviews / Textbooks:
56. Scarani, V., Bechmann-Pasquinucci, H., Cerf, N. J., Dušek, M., Gisin, N., & Peev, M. (2009). The security of practical quantum key distribution. Reviews of Modern Physics, 81(3), 1301. (Comprehensive review of security aspects).
57. Lo, H. K., Curty, M., & Tamaki, K. (2014). Secure quantum key distribution. Nature Photonics, 8(8), 595-604. (Review focusing on security proofs).
58. Gisin, N., Ribordy, G., Tittel, W., & Zbinden, H. (2002). Quantum cryptography. Reviews of Modern Physics, 74(1), 145. (Earlier but still relevant review).

VII. Challenges, Future Prospects, Societal Impact

Error Correction / Fault Tolerance:
59. Shor, P. W. (1995). Scheme for reducing decoherence in quantum computer memory. Physical Review A, 52(4), R2493. (Early work on quantum error correction).
60. Terhal, B. M. (2015). Quantum error correction for quantum memories. Reviews of Modern Physics, 87(2), 307. (Comprehensive review).

Quantum Internet:
61. Wehner, S., Elkouss, D., & Hanson, R. (2018). Quantum internet: A vision for the road ahead. Science, 362(6412). (Vision paper).
62. Kimble, H. J. (2008). The quantum internet. Nature, 453(7198), 1023-1030.

Societal/Ethical:
63. Dowling, J. P., & Milburn, G. J. (2003). Quantum technology: the second quantum revolution. Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society of London. Series A: Mathematical, Physical and Engineering Sciences, 361(1809), 1655-1674. (Discusses broader impact).
64. Check sources like World Economic Forum (WEF) reports or think tank publications discussing geopolitical and economic implications of quantum technology. (Search terms: "quantum computing economic impact," "geopolitics quantum technology").
